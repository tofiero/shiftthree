Help and answers for Airspace Alerts

What is Airspace Alerts?

Airspace Alerts lets you draw a circular area on a map, pick an altitude range, and get a notification the moment an aircraft enters that area. Useful for plane spotters, residents tracking flight activity around their home, photographers chasing specific routes, and anyone curious about what’s flying overhead.

Getting started

  1. Open the app and grant notification permission when prompted — alerts are useless without it.
  2. Go to the Alerts tab and tap Create Alert.
  3. Search for a location or pan the map. Adjust the radius and altitude range.
  4. Save. Turn on Realtime monitoring on the Alerts tab.
  5. You’ll receive a push notification when a matching aircraft enters the area.

Free vs. Paid

The free tier lets you try the app with one alert, small search radius, and 30-minute monitoring sessions. Airspace Alerts Pro removes those limits:

  • Unlimited alerts
  • Large alert areas
  • 24-hour monitoring sessions
  • Full flight history
  • Background monitoring

Monthly: $4.99 with a 3-day free trial. Yearly: $29.99 with a 7-day free trial.

Managing your subscription

Subscriptions are managed by Apple. To view, change, or cancel:

  1. Open the Settings app on your iPhone or iPad.
  2. Tap your name at the top, then Subscriptions.
  3. Find Airspace Alerts in the list.

You can also follow Apple’s full instructions at support.apple.com.

Refunds are handled by Apple — reportaproblem.apple.com.

Troubleshooting

I’m not receiving notifications

  • Confirm notifications are enabled: Settings → Notifications → Airspace Alerts → Allow Notifications on.
  • Confirm Realtime monitoring is toggled on inside the app.
  • Check that the alert you created is enabled (toggle on the alert card).
  • If you’re in a quiet airspace, matches may be infrequent. Try a larger radius or a busier area to verify the system is working.
  • Make sure your device has a working internet connection.

Monitoring stopped on its own

The free tier limits monitoring sessions to 30 minutes. Paid subscribers can run 24-hour sessions and background monitoring. Re-enable monitoring on the Alerts tab to start a new session.

I subscribed but the app still thinks I’m on the free tier

  • Pull down to refresh on the Alerts tab, or fully quit and reopen the app.
  • Open the paywall and tap Restore Purchases.
  • Verify the subscription is active in Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions.
  • If the issue persists, email us with your installation ID (visible in the app’s settings).

Alerts won’t save with a large radius

The free tier caps the alert radius at about 5 km. Larger radii require Airspace Alerts Pro. Tapping Save with a too-large radius opens the upgrade screen.
